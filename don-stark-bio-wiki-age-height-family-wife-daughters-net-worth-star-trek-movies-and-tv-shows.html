Don Stark Biography

Don Stark is an American actor who was born in New York City, New York. He is known for his role as Bob Pinciotti on the Fox Network sitcom That ’70s Show for all eight seasons (1998–2006) and fictional Los Angeles Devils owner Oscar Kinkade in VH1’s Hit the Floor, Star Trek: First Contact (1996), and John Carter in the year 2012. Also, he provided the voice of Vincent in Father of the Pride (2004–2005) and voiced Rhino in Spider-Man (1995-1997).

    Don Stark That 70s Show

    By the time he was featured in That ’70s Show,Stark’s acting career was already going downhill. In 1995, he was cast in the TV sitcom Bless This House as he desperately searched for a career-reviving screen role.

    His involvement in the latest series was not for long as the show was canceled after the first season. It didn’t help matters that he featured in the 1996 movie Santa with Muscles which was rated as “one of the worst Christmas movies of all time.”

    Stark starred alongside Hulk Hogan in the movie. After that, he was in another role that was also a flop in 1997. He was featured in the adaptation of the sci-fi movie Timecop (1997) where he played the character of a commanding officer.

    It was until the next year in 1998, that he struck the right cord in his career with That ’70s Show (1998-2006) where he played the role of Bob Pinciotti. Also, among the cast was Tanya Roberts who played the role of Midge Pinciotti, Bob Pinciotti’s (Don Stark), wife.

    Don Stark Young

    Stark began his career as a student in high school – Cleveland High School – and the circumstances that pushed him to it are quite unnatural.
