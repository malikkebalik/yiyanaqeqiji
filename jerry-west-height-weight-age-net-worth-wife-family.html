Education

Jerry West studied at East Bank High School in East Bank, West Virginia from 1952 to 1956. During his time there, he became the captain of the freshman team and later served as the team’s starting small forward. He also led his school to a state championship and that prompted East Bank High School to change its name to “West Bank High School” every year on March 24 in honor of their basketball prodigy.

After graduating high school, more than 60 universities showed interest in the player and he eventually decided to enroll at the West Virginia University (WVU).

    Jerry West Facts

  • Growing up, he was physically abused by his father and has stated that there was a time when he used to sleep with a loaded shotgun under his bed out of fear that he might have to kill his father in self-defense.
  • An outgoing and aggressive child in his youth, Jerry West’s older brother’s death in 1951 deeply affected him and he became introverted and shy.
  • From 1953 to 1956, he was named All-State and established himself as one of the finest West Virginia high school players of his generation. He also became the state’s first high-school player to score more than 900 points in a season, with an average of 32.2 points per game.
  • Jerry West was portrayed by Jason Clarke in the HBO sports drama series Winning Time: The Rise of the Lakers Dynasty in 2022. In the docuseries, he was shown as a temperamental, foul-mouthed executive prone to angry outbursts and mood swings and West demanded a retraction from the network on April 19, 2022, within two weeks for the “cruel” and “deliberately false” depiction.
  • Throughout his career, Jerry West has been regarded for his extreme work ethic and the NBA has described West as “obsessive perfectionism, unabashed confidence, and an uncompromising will to win… a level of intensity so high it could melt lead”.
