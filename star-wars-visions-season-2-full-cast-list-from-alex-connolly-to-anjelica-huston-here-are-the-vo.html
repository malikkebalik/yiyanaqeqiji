'Star Wars: Visions' Season 2 Full Cast List: From Alex Connolly to Anjelica Huston, here are the vo

Posted by Aldo Pusey on Friday, September 20, 2024

LOS ANGELES, CALIFORNIA: The '' galaxy is expanding once again with a new season of ''. Lucasfilm has confirmed that the second season will feature a wider range of animation styles from all over the world, giving creative talents more control to come up with independent character arcs. Unlike the debut season, the new 'Star Wars: Visions' season will incorporate more global players, showcasing their unique art styles and storytelling abilities.

The series will premiere exclusively on Disney+ on May 4, 2023, and a recent trailer has given us a sneak peek of what's to come. Although the participating countries were announced previously, the animation studios and episode titles were only made public in 2023. From what we've seen in the trailer, each short has a distinct and impressive animation style. At the series' convention panel, the voice cast for each short was also announced. Fans can look forward to seeing some familiar and talented voices bringing the characters to life. With the full titles of each short and their respective animation studios now confirmed, excitement for the new season of 'Star Wars: Visions' is at an all-time high.

Who stars in Disney+'s 'Star Wars: Visions:' Season 2? 

Each episode in the anthology series is made by a different studio, from different parts of the world. The diversity is also evident in the cast list of the animated series as it spans across Spain, Chile, Ireland, United Kingdom, South Korea, France, India, Japan, and South Africa. Let's take a look at the stars of each episode of 'Visions' season 2.

'Sith'

Ursula Corbero and Luis Tosar Rodrigo Blaas.

'Screecher’s Reach'

Alex Connolly, Anjelica Huston, Eva Whitaker, Molly McCann, Niamh Moyles, Noah Rafferty, and Paul Young.

'In the Stars'

Amparo Noguera, Julia Oviedo, Kate Dickie, Valentina Muhr, and Gabriel Osorio.

'I Am Your Mother'

Bebe Cave, Charithra Chandran, Denis Lawson, Maxine Peak, and Magdalena Osinska.

'Journey to the Dark Head'

Choi Soo Min, Chwang Kwang, Jang Ye Na, Lee Kyung Taei, Lee So Young, Lim Chae Hon, Shin Young Woo, Yun Yong Sik, and Hyeong Geun Park.

'The Spy Dancer'

Barbara Weber, Bruce Edward Sheffield, Camille Cottin, Kaycie Chase, Lambert Wilson, Rudi James Jephcott, and Julien Chheng.

'The Bandits of Golak'

Lillete Dubey, Neeraj Kabi, Rajeev Raj, Sonal Kushal, Sumanto Ray, and Ishan Shukla.

'The Pit'

Anika Noni Rose, Cedric Yarbrough, Daveed Diggs, Jordyn Curet, Steven Jay Blum. LeAndre Thomas, and Justin Ridge.

Aau’s Song

Cynthia Erivo, Dineo Du Toti, Faith Baloyi, Milo Jantjie, Tumisho Masha Nadia Darries, and Daniel Clark.
