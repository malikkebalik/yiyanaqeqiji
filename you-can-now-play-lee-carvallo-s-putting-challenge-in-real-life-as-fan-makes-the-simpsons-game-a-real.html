You can now play Lee Carvallo's Putting Challenge in real life as fan makes The Simpsons game a real

Posted by Aldo Pusey on Monday, September 23, 2024

If you are a fan of The Simpsons, especially the 90s variety, then you are almost certainly aware of Lee Carvallo's Putting Challenge.

It was the poor selling game Bart was given in series seven episode 'Marge be not Proud' when in fact he wanted 'Bonestorm'.

Well now - for free - you can play it for real and presumably select a three wood on the putting green.

And it has been endorsed by writer Bill Oakley, who wrote on Twitter: "All Simpsons fans must check out this fantastic PLAYABLE VERSION of Lee Carvallo's Putting Challenge by Aaron Demeter."

In the episode, when Bart attempted to rent Bonestorm from Comic Book Guy's shop, he was told it was out of stock, but there was a shed load of copies of the boring golf game available, which he received as a Christmas present - epic trolling from Marge there.

The instructions from the fictional golfer saw him try and teach Bart how to gently hit the ball on the green.

“Welcome to Lee Carvallo's Putting Challenge. I am Carvallo. Now, choose a club.

"You have chosen a three wood.

"May I suggest a putter?

"Three wood.

"Now enter the force of your swing. I suggest feather touch.

"You have entered 'power drive'. Now, push seven eight seven to swing.
"Ball is in...parking lot. Would you like to play again?

"You have selected, 'No'.”

It's hard to believe nobody had done this before - Surely Bonestorm is next.
