Douglas Wilson Obituary, Member Of Dallas County Hospital District Police Department Died

Posted by Martina Birk on Friday, July 12, 2024
0SHARESShareTweet

Douglas Wilson Obituary, Death – It is with a heavy heart that we announce the passing of Retired Lieutenant Douglas Lynn Wilson, a dedicated and beloved husband, father, grandfather, friend, and police officer. On October 19, 2023, at the age of 78, Lt. Wilson peacefully departed this world, leaving behind a legacy of service and compassion.

Lt. Wilson’s law enforcement career spanned several decades, during which he touched the lives of many. He began his journey with the University Park Police Department and later served with the Highland Park Police Department and the Addison Police Department. In 1997, he joined the Dallas County Hospital District Police Department, where he dedicated over 25 years of his life to service. Within the department, he held supervisory roles in the Communications and Internal Affairs Divisions, contributing significantly to the well-being of the community he served.

In addition to his commitment to law enforcement, Lt. Wilson honorably served his country in the United States Air Force during the Vietnam War, selflessly dedicating his time between 1965 and 1969 to ensure the safety and freedom of his fellow citizens.

Lt. Wilson’s impact on his fellow officers, friends, and the community he served is immeasurable. He was a beacon of strength, compassion, and integrity, leaving an indelible mark on the lives he touched. His memory will be forever treasured and cherished by those who had the privilege of knowing him.

During this time of grief, we extend our deepest condolences to Lt. Wilson’s family, who undoubtedly provided him with unwavering support and love throughout his remarkable life. We share in your sorrow and offer our thoughts and prayers as you navigate this difficult journey of loss.

Details regarding memorial services and arrangements will be shared as they become available. We encourage all those whose lives were touched by Lt. Douglas Wilson to come together to remember and celebrate his remarkable legacy.
