Who is Kelly Moneymaker?

Kelly Moneymaker is an American-renowned singer, songwriter, and music producer.

She is best known as a member of the platinum-selling trio Expose named One of Billboard’s Top 10 Girl Groups of All-Time.

Kelly Moneymaker: Age, Family, Siblings, Ethnicity

The singer Kelly Moneymaker was born on the 4th of June 1970 as Kelly Susan Moneymaker. She hails from Fairbanks, Alaska, USA.

She holds an American nationality and she belongs to Yup’ik descent.

Kelly was born to her father John Moneymaker whereas her mother’s detail is unknown. Likely, her sibling’s details are under the curtain.

In which school did Kelly attend?

Moving toward her academics, Kelly Moneymaker attended Lathrop High School. Later she joined West Valley High School.

Kelly Moneymaker: Professional Life, Career

In her teenage, Kelly Moneymaker moved to Seattle and co-found the band BoyToy along with Michele Rundgren.

A few years later, she moved to California and worked with different popular personalities like Todd Rundgren, Connie Stevens, Enys, Ringo Starr, and many more.

During that time, her mutual friend introduced her to Jeanette Jurado, and was invited to join Expose after Gioia Bruno’s departure due to a medical condition.

Afterward, Moneymaker started performing on the Expose and launched LP albums like “I Wish The Phone Would Ring”, “I’ll Never Get Over You (Getting Over Me)”, “In Walked Love” and “As Long As I Can Dream”.

In 1996, Kelly Moneymaker pursued solo projects with Arista Records. Then, she released several independent albums Stone LP (2015), Race Against The Sky (2013), Like a Blackbird LP (1996), Through The Basement Walls LP (2003), and so on.

Her single “Can’t Live Without Your Love” reached #1 on the New Music Weekly AC40 single charts. The songs also became the theme song for NBC’s Days of Our Lives.

Due to the success of “Can’t Live”, she co-wrote and co-produced the Love Song LP for Days of Our Lives.
