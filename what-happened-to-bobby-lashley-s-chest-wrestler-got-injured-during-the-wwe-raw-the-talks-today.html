Who is Bobby Lashley?

Bobby Lashley is one of the few rookies to make a big impact in a major wrestling company like WWE (Formerly WWF). He is known for his suplexes, especially his bell-to-belly suplexes, which are hard to do and soft to say.

Lashley Lashley went to college at Missouri Valley College, where he won three national titles from 1996 to 1998. Lashley was the NAIA wrestling champion from 1997 to 1998. He weighed 177 lbs. back then. Lashley then started training hard and gained 100 pounds of muscle. He now weighs between 273 and 279 pounds.

In November 2003, World Wrestling Entertainment (WWE) signed Lashley to a contract, and he spent some time in the WWE’s farm federation, Ohio Valley Wrestling. During his time at OVW, he was known as Blaster Lashley and was part of the stable “Bolin Services” with Mike Bondo (aka “Michael Brenley”) and “Keny Doane,” who both now work for WWE as part of the Spirit Squad stable.

Lashley then made his TV debut on “Friday Night Smackdown” against Simon Dean under the name Bobby Lashley or just “Lashley.” Lashley won and impressed everyone by doing four push-ups with Simon Dean on his back. He quickly became one of the most watched and popular rookies. He also fought Dean in Lashley’s No Mercy 2005 pay-per-view match. The loser of this match has to eat 20 cheeseburgers. Then, Lashley put the burgers down Dean’s throat.

Early years

Franklin Roberto Lashley was born on July 16, 1976, in Junction City, Kansas. His father was a drill sergeant in the U.S. Army. Lashley’s family is from Panama. He was raised by a single mother who barely spoke English and had a hard time getting a good job. As of 2015, one of his three older sisters was still serving in the U.S. Air Force. The family moved from one military base to another every so often. While his dad was stationed at Fort Riley in Kansas, Lashley went to Fort Riley Middle School. In seventh grade, he learned about amateur wrestling as an alternative to football during the off-season. He later got his diploma from Junction City High School, where he also kept wrestling. He went to Missouri Valley College, where he won the NAIA National Wrestling Championship in the 177-pound division in 1996, 1997, and 1998. He got his bachelor’s degree in human-service agency management and recreation administration in 1999.

Lashley when he was serving in the US Army.
After college, he joined the U.S. Army like his father did, and he kept wrestling as an amateur in the army’s World Class Athlete Program. During his three years in the Army, he won a gold and a silver medal in senior freestyle wrestling at the International Military Sports Council. Lashley was living and training in Colorado Springs in 2003 with the goal of making Team USA so he could wrestle in the 2004 Olympics. While there, he saw a bank robbery and had to duck to avoid gunfire, which hurt his knee and ended his amateur career.

Career in professional wrestling

In January 2005, Lashley started working as Blaster Lashley for WWE’s development territory Ohio Valley Wrestling. In the middle of 2005, Lashley wrestled in dark matches for both Raw and SmackDown! at four live World Wrestling Entertainment (WWE) events. On September 23, 2005, he made his TV debut as a face on SmackDown! under his real name. He was introduced as a three-time National Amateur Wrestling Champion, four-time All-American, two-time Armed Forces Champion, and 2002 Silver Medalist at the Military World Championship. In his first match, he beat Simon Dean. He also beat Dean in his first pay-per-view match on October 9 at No Mercy. On the November 11 episode of SmackDown!, Lashley beat Orlando Jordan in a qualifying match for a spot on Team SmackDown! at Survivor Series on November 27. At Survivor Series, he was the first person to be eliminated, but his team won in the end.

Lashley was the first SmackDown! wrestler to qualify for that year’s Royal Rumble on January 6, 2006. Lashley was the eighth wrestler to enter the Royal Rumble on January 29. He beat Sylvain, but Big Show and Kane, who were the World Tag Team Champions for Raw at the time, took him out. After the Royal Rumble, Lashley and John “Bradshaw” Layfield (JBL) started fighting. This led to a match at No Way Out on February 19, where Lashley lost his first singles match, mostly because Finlay got in the way. This led to a feud between Lashley and Finlay that lasted all of February and March. On the March 17 episode of SmackDown!, Finlay used a shillelagh to win a lumberjack match against Lashley.

The World Wrestling Entertainment (2004-2008)

Lashley signed a developmental contract with World Wrestling Entertainment (WWE) in 2004. He then trained under the ring name Blaster Lashely in the WWE’s developmental territory at the time, Ohio Valley Wrestling (OVW). He was part of the stable Bolin Services while he was in OVW. Lashley’s size and athleticism helped him win a string of matches in OVW and after he started appearing on RAW and Smackdown in 2005. In the same year, Lashley won his first singles match on Pay-Per-View (PPV) at No Mercy on October 9, beating Simon Dean. In the last few months of 2005 and into the beginning of the next year, Lashley continued to win impressive singles matches and other matches with their own rules.

United States Champion/Fight with Booker T (2006)

Lashley took part in the King of the Ring tournament on April 21, 2006. This was the first King of the Ring tournament to be held in four years, since Brock Lesnar won the last one in 2002. On Friday Night Smackdown on April 22, Lashley beat Mark Henry by count-out in the first round. On the May 9 episode of Smackdown, he moved on to face Finlay and beat him. This put him in the final round of the King of the Ring against Booker T. On May 21, at Judgment Day, Booker T. beat Lashley. After winning the King of the King, Booker started wrestling as King Booker, which was the start of his “heel run.”
