Lilia Vu Bio (Wiki)

Posted by Jenniffer Sheldon on Wednesday, July 31, 2024

Who Is Lilia Vu?

Lilia Vu is an American professional female golfer, who has won three titles on the Symetra Tour.

She has won the 2023 Chevron Championship and the 2023 Women’s British Open.

Lilia Vu- Bio, Age, Parents, Siblings, Ethnicity, Education

Golfer Lilia Vu was born on October 14, 1997, in Fountain Valley, California, United States. As of 2023, her age is 26.

Similarly, she holds an American nationality and is of Asian-American ethnicity.

Her parents are named, Kieu Thuy and Douglas Vu but their profession is unknown. She is also yet to confirm whether she has any siblings or not.

Education

As per her academics, she completed her graduation from Fountain Valley High School and UCLA (University of California, Los Angeles).

Lilia Vu- Professional Life, Careers

Lilia won the 2018 Curtis Cup with four points and the 2018 Espirito Santo Trophy, while making a successful appearance for the U.S. National team.  Again she became the winner of the 2018 Arnold Palmer Cup at Evian Resort Golf Club based in France.

In 2015, she joined UCLA and received the 2016 Pac-12 Conference Freshman of the Year. Following that, she became the WGCA Player of the Year, Pac-12 Conference Golfer of the Year, and Honda Award finalist. Then, she was placed first on the all-time UCLA career, earning eight individual victory titles.

As an amateur, she played in the U.S. Women’s Open and the ANA Inspiration.

After turning professional in January 2019, Lilia Vu made one cut in nine starts, to make her place for the 2019 LPGA Tour.

For the first time, she got three titles on the Symetra Tour and was placed in the top 250 in the Women’s World Golf Rankings. Along with her victory in the Garden City Charity Classic, the Twin Bridges Championship, and the Four Winds Invitation, she added the 2021 Potawatomi Cup and bonus prize money.

Defeating Angle Yin in the 2023 Chevron Championship, she again beat Charly Hull at the 2023 Women’s British Open.

During her long professional career, she has earned various achievements and awards including,

  • 2018 Women’s Golf Coaches Association (WGCA)
  • Player of the Year 2018 Annika Award winner
  • 2018 First-Team All-American
  • 2017 Second-Team All-American
  • 2017 Pac-12 Freshman of the Year
  • 2017 First-Team All-Pac-12
  • 2017 WGCA All-American Honorable Mention
  • 2016 AJGA Rolex Junior All-American First Team
  • 2015 AJGA Rolex Junior All-American Second Team
  • Member of the 2018 United States Curtis Cup team.

Lilia Vu- Net worth
